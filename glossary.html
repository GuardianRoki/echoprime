Tune Break System

Tune Break

Tune Break

A combat mechanic introduced in version 3.0. Certain Resonator attacks accumulate Off-Tune Level on eligible enemies. Once an enemy's Off-Tune Level is full, they enter the Mistune state briefly. While the enemy is in Mistune, an indicator appears when the Resonator attacks or dodges. Pressing the prompt performs a Tune Break, interrupting the enemy, clearing their Off-Tune Level, and forcing them into the Tune Disruption state. Some enemy skills or phases make them immune to Off-Tune accumulation and prevent entering Mistune, though this immunity is removed if they are already in the Mistune state when it triggers. Some enemies can only enter Mistune while casting certain skills or in a specific state.

Resonance Mode

Tune Break

Resonators without a Resonance Mode may only perform a standard Tune Break on a target in Mistune. Resonators with a Resonance Mode operate in either Tune Rupture or Tune Strain. While in Tune Rupture, the Resonator applies Tune Rupture - Shifting to targets. While in Tune Strain, they apply Tune Strain - Shifting. When a target carrying one of these effects enters Tune Disruption via Tune Break, they enter the Tune Rupture - Interfered or Tune Strain - Interfered state respectively. Resonators that respond to these states trigger additional effects when attacking targets in that state. Tune Rupture - Interfered lasts 8 seconds and Tune Strain - Interfered lasts 30 seconds.

Off-Tune Buildup Rate

Tune Break

A stat that increases the rate at which a Resonator accumulates Off-Tune Level on enemies. Higher Off-Tune Buildup Rate means enemies enter the Mistune state more frequently, enabling more Tune Breaks per fight.

Tune Break Boost

Tune Break

A stat that increases the damage and effectiveness of Tune Break and the Interfered states that follow it. Referenced in the Reel of Spliced Memories sonata set and in Lynae's kit.

Status Effects

Havoc Bane

Status Effect

A Havoc debuff that reduces the target's DEF by 2% per stack, up to 3 stacks. Higher stacks mean greater DEF reduction, amplifying all damage the target takes.

Aero Erosion

Status Effect

An Aero debuff that deals periodic Aero DMG to the target. Stacks up to 3 times by default. More stacks mean more frequent or stronger damage ticks.

Spectro Frazzle

Status Effect

A Spectro debuff that periodically deals Spectro DMG to the target. The damage scales upward as the stack count increases, rewarding sustained application.

Glacio Chafe

Status Effect

A Glacio debuff that deals Glacio DMG when inflicted. Stacks up to 10 by default. Higher stacks deal more damage and reduce the target's movement speed. At maximum stacks all stacks are consumed to freeze the target. Struggling accelerates recovery from the frozen state.

Electro Flare

Status Effect

An Electro debuff that deals periodic damage to the target. Stacks up to 10 by default. The target loses half its current stacks with each damage instance. Higher stacks deal more damage, so the goal is to keep stacks as high as possible.

Fusion Burst

Status Effect

A Fusion debuff that stacks up to 10 by default. Higher stacks deal more damage. When max stacks are reached, all stacks are consumed to trigger an explosion dealing Fusion DMG to nearby targets.
